(Soya)Chaap Menu


Ingredients:
  • 200 grams paneer (cottage cheese), cubed
    		•
  • 2 tablespoons butter
    		•
  • 2 tablespoons oil
    		•
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
    		•
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
    		•
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
    		•
  • 1 tablespoon cashew nuts, soaked
    		•
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
    		•
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    		•
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
    		•
  • 1/2 cup cream
    		•
  • 1/2 cup water
    		•
  • salt to taste
    		•
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
    		•
Method:
Step 1 - Preparing the gravy:
In a pan, heat 1 tablespoon of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Add ginger garlic paste and sauté until raw smell disappears. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.

Step 2 - Making the paste:
In a blender, blend soaked cashew nuts with a little water to make a smooth paste. Add this paste to the gravy. Mix well.

Step 3 - Adding spices:
Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.

Step 4 - Cooking the paneer:
In another pan, heat the remaining butter and oil. Add cubed paneer and sauté until lightly golden. Remove and keep aside.

Step 5 - Combining everything:
Add the sautéed paneer to the gravy. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the gravy. Mix gently.

Step 6 - Finishing touches:
Pour cream into the gravy and mix well. Add water to adjust the consistency. Simmer for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.

Step 7 - Serving:
Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
