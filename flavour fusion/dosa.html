(D)osa Menu


Ingredients:
  • 1 cup dosa rice (also known as parboiled rice)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
    		•
  • 1/4 cup poha (flattened rice)
    		•
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
    		•
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
    		•
  • Water, as needed (for soaking and grinding)
    		•
Method:
Step 1 - Soaking:
Wash dosa rice, urad dal, chana dal, poha, and fenugreek seeds thoroughly and soak them in water for 4-6 hours. Soaking helps in easy grinding and fermentation.

Step 2 - Grinding:
After soaking, drain the water and grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter. Use a wet grinder or a blender to achieve a smooth consistency. Add water gradually as needed to facilitate the grinding process. The batter should be thick yet pourable.

Step 3 - Fermentation:
Transfer the ground batter into a large bowl. Cover it with a lid or cloth and place it in a warm place for fermentation. Allow it to ferment for about 8-12 hours or overnight. Fermentation helps in developing the characteristic tangy flavor and light texture of dosas.

Step 4 - Making dosas:
Once the batter is fermented, mix it well to incorporate any settled solids. Heat a dosa pan or griddle over medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the pan and spread it evenly in a circular motion to form a thin layer. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until the bottom surface turns golden brown and crisp. Flip the dosa and cook the other side until it is cooked through and lightly browned.

Step 5 - Serving:
Serve hot dosas immediately with coconut chutney, sambar, or any desired accompaniment. Dosas are traditionally enjoyed for breakfast or as a snack.
